A publication designed for the Central Saint Martins degree show. The layout mimics the experience of visiting the degree show - a continuous flow of consciousness. A fluid layout with an invisible grid in between the pages help create a unified publication of a diverse range of work. To enhance clarity, the students’ work is divided into 8 chapters titled as questions to guide the reader through the experience. Each question starts with a selected dissertation excerpt that gives further context to that section.


Art-direction / Design / Print

* The transparency of the sleeve implies ‘intangibility’, which reflects modern craft media like code. Transparency is also key to the political aspects of modern craft; open-source values the transparent sharing of information.

The ‘Villard diagram’ provides the framework for layout. William Morris returned to this diagram for his work. As many consider Morris the ‘grandfather’ of craft, the Villard diagram became a perfect starting point for layout. Symmetrical spreads created through this grid imply balance and equality, reflecting Morris’ opinions of craft.

* The reversed page numbers imply a return to origins, which captures elements of nostalgia within craft.
